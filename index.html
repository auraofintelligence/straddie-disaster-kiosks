Minjerribah resilience concept

Straddie Disaster Kiosks.

Community Sovereign Kiosks are proposed offline-first local nodes for emergency communication, community information, digital access, and resilience planning on North Stradbroke Island / Minjerribah.

The idea is simple at the front: useful public touchpoints that can keep working when power, internet, ferries, phones, or mainland systems are under stress. Under the hood, the concept can grow into solar power, mesh communications, local data storage, digital twin access, and community coordination.

What the kiosks may support

Emergency access

Local alerts, offline messages, maps, coordination notes, and basic community information during disruptions.

Everyday usefulness

Device charging, local notices, digital literacy, visitor information, community services, and training touchpoints.

Future resilience

Mesh networks, digital twin data, community records, microgrid support, and locally governed public infrastructure.

Current status

This is a concept and pitch page, not a live emergency service. The next useful step is scoping: locations, partners, rough costs, public value, grant pathways, and a safe pilot kiosk.
